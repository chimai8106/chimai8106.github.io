About Me

I'm a Vietnamese student studying Computer Science and Mathematics at Colby College, where I'm a Pulver Science Scholar. I grew up in Nghe An Province and went to Phan Boi Chau High School for the Gifted before coming to the US for college.

I love building things, whether it's full-stack applications, machine learning models, or legos. Currently, I'm researching ML and human feature integration, and I recently built gastronome, an AI-powered recipe app that extracts recipes from videos. I competed in math olympiads back in Vietnam and still get excited when I find an elegant proof or efficient algorithm.

Outside of academic, I founded the Colby Baking Club and used to run an online bakery back in Vietnam. There's something satisfying about the precision required in both coding and baking: measure twice, debug once. When I'm not at my computer or in the kitchen, you'll find me outdoors. I'm a Trip Leader for Colby Outing Club and Colby Outdoor Orientation, leading hiking trips. I also love swimming. Swimming and being on the water help me think through problems differently than staring at code ever could.

I'm still figuring out how all these pieces fit together but I'm excited to see where the intersection of technology, creativity, and community takes me.

Research

Current

Machine Learning & Human Feature Integration

Department of Computer Science, Colby College • Feb 2025 - Present

Research Question: How do we optimally train machine learning models when only a subset of features is available at train time, especially when incorporating user-provided "human features" that are expensive to collect?

I'm investigating adaptive querying strategies that determine which features to request from users at test time to maximize model accuracy while minimizing user burden. My work focuses on training strategies under feature scarcity—comparing random selection, uncertainty-based approaches, and intelligent heuristic methods.

Key Contributions

🔧
Adaptive Querying Framework

Developed information-theoretic feature selection algorithms leveraging entropy-based metrics to dynamically determine optimal feature queries, achieving comparable accuracy to full-feature models while reducing user burden by up to 60%

🔧
Systematic Strategy Analysis

Designed and implemented 6 masking strategies (exact random, probabilistic, uncertainty-based, heuristic) with controlled experimental conditions, conducting comprehensive train-test alignment analysis across semi-realistic datasets to identify optimal deployment configurations

🔧
End-to-End Evaluation Pipeline

Architected scalable experimental infrastructure in Python integrating predictive modeling (scikit-learn), statistical analysis (NumPy/pandas), and visualization tools to evaluate performance across varying feature availability scenarios with rigorous cross-validation
